<h2>HYSS Homework Policy</h2>
<p>Our homework policy articulates the purpose of homework and how homework
load is regulated, monitored and coordinated. This policy is communicated
to staff, parents and students.</p>
<h4>Definition of Homework (in view of Future of Learning initiative)</h4>
<p>Homework refers to any learning activity <strong>in curricular subjects</strong> that
schools require students to complete <strong>outside of </strong>curriculum
time, regardless of the mode of delivery (i.e. pen-and-paper, virtual/online).&nbsp;</p>
<p>Although student-initiated tasks are generally not deemed as homework
(see examples below), the school is mindful of students’ overall load and
the impact on their well-being when designing homework and setting deadlines.</p>

<h5>Purpose of Homework</h5>
<p>Homework, when used purposefully, can reinforce learning. It enables students
to ascertain their progress in learning, and for teachers to consider students’
readiness and learning needs, and provide feedback to move learning forward
with timely intervention and support when necessary.</p>
<p>Homework enables students to:</p>
<p>1 . apply knowledge and skills learnt from class</p>
<p>2 . receive feedback that moves learning forward</p>
<p>3 . reinforce learning</p>
<p>4 . cultivate a healthy disposition towards ownership of learning.</p>

<h5>Roles and Responsibilities of Stakeholders</h5>
<p>Educators, parents and students play an important role in ensuring that
homework serves its intended purpose.</p>
<p><strong>Subject Teachers</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>support students in the completion of homework</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>provide timely feedback for all homework assigned with appropriate intervention
to facilitate student’s learning (e.g. mark individual question responses,
provide comments on work based on rubrics shared with students beforehand,
leverage AI-enabled feedback for digital learning tasks like SLS, review
questions as a class while students self-/peer-mark)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>engage parents on homework matters and encourage parents to provide suitable
home support and supervision for students’ learning</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Form Teachers</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>include homework management as part of one-to-one Teacher-Student-Interaction
(TSI) sessions and class feedback sessions</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>give feedback to respective subject teachers on homework given</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>engage parents on homework matters and encourage parents to provide suitable
home support and supervision for students’ learning&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>House Masters &amp; Year Heads</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>monitor and support students who have difficulties with homework despite
interventions by Subject Teachers and/or Form Teachers</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>discuss during House meetings and support teachers with strategies and
interventions for students who have difficulties with homework completion</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>TLSC &amp; IP Heads</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>review and refine homework policy based on teachers’ feedback</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>coordinate homework across departments, streamlining load during peak
periods to balance academic workload and co-curricular demands (e.g. competitions
like NSG and SYF)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>coordinate across departments to set a balanced homework load during public
and school holidays to ensure student well-being while maintaining academic
rigour</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>engage parents on homework matters and provide information on students’
learning progress for recalcitrant cases</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>School Leaders</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>review homework guidelines and guide staff to implement homework policies</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Parents</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>set regular study time each day for revision and completion of homework</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>provide supervision for homework when necessary</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>help child establish a study area, with good light, space and which is
free from distractions</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>support and assist when child has difficulties in completing homework</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>partner with school to update to discuss, clarify any doubts and support
child’s learning and development</p>
<p></p>
</li>
</ul>
