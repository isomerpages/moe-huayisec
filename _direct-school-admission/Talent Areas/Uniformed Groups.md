<h2>Uniformed Groups</h2>
<p><em>DSA Talent Areas in Uniformed Groups (for 2026):</em>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>National Cadet Corps (NCC) Land (Boys)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>National Police Cadet Crops (NPCC) (Boys &amp; Girls)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Red Cross Youth (Boys &amp; Girls)</p>
<p></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Hua Yi Secondary School offers its DSA under Uniformed Groups (UG) and
is focused on developing&nbsp;leadership opportunities in the UG. Hua Yians
in the UG will enjoy opportunities to develop skills, dispositions, and
talents to equip them with the skills and support required to lead others
as well as to serve the community.</p>

<p><strong>Highlights</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Emplaced on a mentoring programme to support their transition into possible
leadership roles.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Priority for select leadership opportunities and skills training to equip
them with the knowledge to facilitate their peers’ learning in the UG.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Exposure to varied leadership development experiences at HQ level and
school.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Opportunities to interact with UG leaders at cluster and HQ level.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Criteria</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Students should submit as part of their application documents that attest
to their experience in any Primary School UG.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students should possess personal qualities and strengths that are indicative
of their potential to be a UG cadet. This can be demonstrated using their
CCA records, a personal statement and character referees.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Shortlisted students will be interviewed by a panel to determine If they
will be a good fit for the school.</p>
</li>
</ul>
