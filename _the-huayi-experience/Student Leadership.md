<h2>Student Leadership</h2>
<p>Through stringent selection, appointment and training, Hua Yians are nurtured
to develop their leadership competencies and be stretched to develop their
potential in one of eight leadership groups. The roles of these eight groups
are as follows:</p>
<p><strong>1 . Student Council</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Represent the student body in matters pertaining to student welfare;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Promote innovative changes and improvement in school for students;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Organise key school-wide events which promote stronger camaraderie and
school spirit;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Collaborate with community partners for meaningful community involvement
work;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Administer school duties and maintain general discipline.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>2 . ONE Advocates</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Advocate National Education(NE) and citizenship values;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Initiate and plan key NE commemorative events including International
Friendship Day, Total Defence Day, Racial Harmony Day, and National Day
Celebration;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Promote and forge strong friendships with international students.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>3 . Class Leaders</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Oversee the functions of the Class Committee, including administering
of daily class duties;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Inculcate strong teamwork and class spirit;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Create a caring and safe class environment that enhances learning and
development; and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Plan, organise and provide support for key school events such as Singapore
Kindness Movement and Teachers’ Day Celebrations.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>4 . CCA Leaders</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Lead in the respective Co-Curricular Activities (CCAs) and inspire Hua
Yians to achieve their best; and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Initiate, plan and organise programmes, events, bonding activities and
Values-in-Action (VIA) projects for the CCA.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>5 . House Leaders</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Take the lead in fostering a strong House identity and school identity
within Hua Yians; and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Plan, organise and provide support for key House events such as House
activities and events, and the Senior-Junior Mentorship programme.</p>
<p></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>6 . Peer Support Leaders</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Develop a strong peer support culture in Hua Yi (“Hua Yi Cares”) through
the following:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Peer bonding: fostering bonds of friendship and sense of belonging to
the class and school;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Peer helping: providing practical help and social and emotional support
to peers in need or in distress;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Peer influencing: promoting positive mindset and mental well-being, role-modelling
pro-social behaviour both online and offline;</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>Plan, organise and provide support for cohort / school-wide events related
to peer support and relationships, including Mental Health and Cyber Wellness.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>7 . ICT Champs</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Provide technical support for both teachers and their classmates such
as assisting teachers in the set-up of AVA equipment and IT resources;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Ensure the maintenance and safe-keeping of AVA equipment;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Immediately report damages or breakdowns in AVA equipment and facilities;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Assist in providing feedback to the ICT Department on issues or ideas
which can be adopted in the classroom;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Provide supportive services for students and teachers in the use of Apple
devices.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>8 . Environment Ambassadors</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Foster sustainable environmental habits in our school community;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Take the initiative to represent the school in national environmental
competitions and school workshops;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Assist the school in investigating data needed to help the school community
be more energy-efficient, reduce waste and conserve water.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p>Hua Yi’s Student Leadership DSA Outreach Workshop allows Primary 6 participants
to better understand how to develop their leadership skills through interactions
with student leaders from Hua Yi. They are also able to foster new relationships
in a safe environment and build self-confidence for their eventual entry
to any secondary school.</p>
